Find out 3 amazing places in Busan

Busan's attractions range from centuries-old Buddhist temples dotting the city's mountains and coastline to pristine beaches with crystal-clear waters. This coastal city offers plenty to do year-round - families can spend time at a seaside aquarium, shoppers can explore vibrant neighborhoods, and nature lovers can enjoy long hikes to scenic viewpoints. The Buddhist sanctuaries along Busan's coast and mountains feature impressive architecture that will delight photographers.

1. Haedong Yonggungsa Temple

Haedong Yonggungsa Temple is a Buddhist temple located in the northeasternmost part of Busan. Built in 1376, it is one of the few temples in Korea constructed by the seaside – you can enjoy views of the East Sea on one side and beautiful mountains on the other.

Good for:

- History

2. Beomeo-sa Temple

Beomeosa Temple is one of the largest sanctuaries in South Korea. It is situated high on the eastern edge of Geumjeong Mountain, far from the hustle and bustle of the city. The temple's Daeungjeon Hall is a well-preserved example of Joseon Dynasty architecture.

Good for:

- History

3. Yongdusan Park

Yongdusan Park, located in the center of Busan, is home to some of the city's most important monuments. You can enjoy spectacular views from the top of the 120-meter-high Busan Tower. The park has two museums – check out traditional musical instruments at the Museum of World Folk Instruments and over 80 Korean sailing ships at the Exhibition Hall of World Model Boats.

Good for:

- Couples

- Families

- Budget

The best things to do in Busan showcase the city's reputation as a major seaport in Asia. Often seen as the essence of South Korea, you'll experience a unique atmosphere in terms of ethnic and cultural diversity, as the city welcomes a cosmopolitan crowd year-round.
